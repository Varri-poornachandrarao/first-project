Surya S/O Krishnan

Director:Gautham Vasudev Menon

Hero:Surya

The story begins with Major Surya Krishnan, a young Indian Army officer, receiving news of his father Krishnan’s sudden death while on a rescue mission in Kashmir. As he mourns, he reflects on his life through a series of flashbacks. The narrative traces Krishnan’s life from his college days at Madras Christian College in the 1970s, where he falls in love with Malini, marries her, and raises two children—Surya and Shriya. Krishnan is portrayed as a loving, supportive father who deeply influences his son’s values and life choices.

Surya, as a young man, meets Meghna on a train journey and falls in love. Though she leaves for California to pursue her studies, Krishnan encourages him to follow her. After Meghna dies in the Oklahoma City bombing, Surya descends into depression, turning to drugs and alcohol. His father, though silent, supports him emotionally, eventually sending him on a journey to Kashmir to find himself. There, Surya rescues a kidnapped boy, regains his strength, and finds purpose.

He later meets Priya, Shriya’s best friend, who has loved him since childhood. Their relationship blossoms, and Surya eventually joins the Indian Army. After marrying Priya, he learns his father is terminally ill. Despite the emotional turmoil, Surya completes his mission and returns to honor his father’s passing. The film ends with Surya and his family performing last rites, symbolizing closure and the enduring legacy of fatherhood.
